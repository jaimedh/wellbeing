My Story

The Big One, Called Life

The Beginning

It wasn’t until I was grown that I realized that I wasn’t at all unique.  As a child I felt very unique but not in a good way, more like an alien. 

I was born with a rare disease, I had a hole right in the middle of my abdomen.  A lot of my insides grew exposed outside my body.  When I was born, the doctors told my parents to go home, not to get attached, as I wouldn’t make it through the week.  Well they didn’t give up on me and I didn’t give up on life.  I survived surgery and made an amazing recovery.  It left me with a big scar that runs deeper then my physique. 

Quake to the Core

My insides have nerve damage that range from the equivalency of a sun burn to 3rd degree burns.  As nerves are attached to everything, when my nerves misfire it effects my entire body.  I get these excruciating attacks that rock me from my core, reeking havoc on my entire Being.   Sometimes they come on so quickly that I’m left stunned.

My only choice is to hold on and wait for the internal quake to stop, it could be an hour or several days.  Then it takes time to recover. 

As a kid my attacks were minimal.  In my twenties they were mostly manageable. In my early 30s I felt like I had it under control.  Yoga became my secret to life!  I had a strong practice.  I became a certified yoga teacher specializing in yoga for persistent pain.  I was teaching wellness workshops combining my yoga training with my Indigenous teachings.  I had built a solid foundation and found balance. 

I taught all over BC, for different Indigenous gatherings, as well as at a local yoga studio.  I felt like I had a purpose and that all my pain and suffering had not been in vain. 

Crumbling Down

On a beautiful fall day my life came crumbling down.  I had an attack that was so bad I ended up in the hospital.  Because my disease is so rare they didn’t know what was wrong or how to help.  I was so sick that there are days I don’t remember.  My husband didn’t think I would make it home alive.  Again I didn’t give up on life.  I survived!

My attack lasted for months with little change.  I couldn’t really eat; I lost a lot of weight.  My organs and body were not functioning properly.  In the beginning weeks I practiced every healing modality I knew.  I prayed, seen Elders and energy healers.  I went to every doctor that would see me.  My internal quake was relentless and my entire Being was tired.  Nothing was working.  With all my might I couldn’t stop it.

Conclusion, Just Hold On

My husband would tell me in my hardest moments all I had to do was breathe.  So easy to say, but not so easy to do.

What I have come to realize is that my specifics are my own but I am not unique at all.  Everyone has their story; we all have scars. Everyone has Life Challenges that quake and rock them to their core. It can creep up slowly or quickly knock you off-balance.  It sometimes lasts an hour or seems to go on forever.  When it is happening all we can do is hold on and breathe!  We can’t quake forever.
